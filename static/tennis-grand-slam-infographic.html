Case Study

Tennis Grand Slam Infographic

My role

Creative Direction
Infographic Design

Client

Self initiative project

Year

2018

Challenge

Visualized and categorized the data for tennis Grand Slam after open era. Including player’s sex, nationality, one-handed backhand or two-handed backhand, and the amount of time of winning Grand Slam.

Research

I came out with eight different data levels, including four Grand Slam, year of the competition, player’s gender, age, nationality, right-handed or left-handed, one-handed backhand or two-handed backhand and different surface of the courts. I found the Grand Slam’s championship each year from ATP and WTA website, then I started to collect data after 1968, which is the beginning of tennis’s open era.

Result

After many hours of work, I came out with three different diagrams showing player’s age, right/left-handed, one/two-handed backhand, player’s nationality, amount of time of winning each Grand Slam and time of each player’s winning trophy time categorized by their nationality.

Conclusion

Although this whole project took me so much time and I was struggling and frustrating during all these complicated process, I still enjoy making the infographic design a lot. The main reason that I love doing infographic design is because it’s very challenging, instead of struggling of running out of creative ideas like the normal design projects, the infographic also become very challenging in terms of collecting and organizing the large amount of data.I was struggling to come out with the answer that how to use all these data I collected in one diagram during the time.
